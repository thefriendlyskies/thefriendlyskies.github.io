The cabin crew is one of the most crucial aspects of a flight. Their friendliness and efficiency determine the overall passenger experience, and may significantly affect an airline’s reputation. Let us learn more about the cabin crew, and the top airlines that are known to have the best cabin crew in the world.

## What is a Cabin Crew?

Cabin crew, also known as flight attendants, are trained professionals responsible for the safety and comfort of passengers during commercial flights. Passengers may be familiar seeing them serving meals and tending to in-flight needs. However, the cabin crew’s primary duty is ensuring safety, including conducting pre-flight checks, demonstrating emergency equipment, and being prepared for emergency situations where evacuation may be needed. 

As the cabin crew should work in an enclosed aircraft cabin, often across time zones and at high altitudes, they are required to maintain calmness and show quick decision-making abilities at all times. This is achieved through rigorous training, where they learn to master emergency procedures, first aid, survival techniques, alongside interpersonal skills to assist passengers and keep them comfortable in any situation. Cabin crew should show a unique blend of professionalism, attentiveness, and hospitality, as their service affects passenger’s overall experience as well as the airline’s reputation.

Now, let us dive into 10 airlines known to have the best cabin crew in the world.

## 1. Singapore Airlines

{{< img src="/06042026/1.png" id="i_plane" caption="Rolf Wallner / Wikimedia" alt="Singapore Airlines Airbus A380 (9V-SKJ) takes off at Zurich Airport.">}}  
   
Topping the list is [Singapore Airlines](https://www.singaporeair.com/en_UK/br/flying-withus/our-story/), an airline known for cabin crew excellence. It has claimed the [SKYTRAX](https://www.worldairlineawards.com/worlds-best-airline-cabin-crew-2025/) title for World's Best Cabin Crew in 2025, alongside the Best in Asia award. This reputation is earned through an extraordinary commitment to their cabin crew training.   
   
While most airlines conduct six-to-eight-week courses focused primarily on safety, Singapore Airlines puts its recruits through an intensive nearly four-month program. In batches of just 20 trainees, future crew members learn comprehensive safety protocols, aircraft-specific drills, and emergency procedures including simulated ditchings.   
   
They are also immersed in deportment, grooming, cultural sensitivity, wine appreciation, and communication techniques. This approach ensures they can handle everything from non-English speaking passengers to in-flight emergencies with the [poise and efficiency](https://thefriendlyskies.net/article/inflight-wifi/) that has become the airline's hallmark.

## 2. ANA All Nippon Airways

{{< img src="/06042026/2.png" id="i_plane" caption="Aero Icarus from Zürich, Switzerland / Wikimedia" alt="All Nippon Airways Boeing 747-281B.">}}  
   
Next is [ANA All Nippon Airways](https://cabincrew24.com/how-to-become-cabin-crew-with-ana-all-nippon-airways/#google_vignette), Japan's prestigious 5-star carrier. It has earned its reputation for cabin crew excellence by upholding the art of Japanese hospitality in its training. Operating an extensive network spanning over 80 international and 110 domestic destinations, ANA also ensures its flight attendants are immersed in an intensive training program in Japan that goes far beyond standard emergency procedures and service skills.   
   
What sets them apart is the exclusive training in Omotenashi, the unique Japanese philosophy of wholehearted hospitality, which instills a genuine anticipation of passengers' needs and an attention to detail that makes every interaction remarkable. This cultural foundation, combined with rigorous safety preparation, allows ANA's cabin crew to deliver the kind of intuitive, hospitable service that has consistently earned the airline its high ratings among travelers who appreciate thoughtful in-flight care.

## 3. Cathay Pacific 

{{< img src="/06042026/3.png" id="i_plane" caption="MarcelX42 / Wikimedia" alt="Cathay Pacific Airbus A321.">}}

As Hong Kong's leading premium airline and its flag carrier, [Cathay Pacific](https://careers.cathaypacific.com/en/careers/our-teams/flight-attendant) ensures that its cabin crew exemplify the airline's core values: Thoughtful, Progressive, and Can-do. They deliver exceptional service while maintaining the rigorous safety standards for which the Hong Kong-based carrier is known. Their training and annual recurrent assessments takes place in a dedicated facility adjacent to the airline's headquarters, just floors above where pilots are trained. 

This [training](https://www.cathaypacific.com/cx/en_HK/inspiration/cathay-stories/cathay-pacific-cabin-crew-safe-training.html) emphasizes the critical nature of their role in emergencies. Cabin mock-ups equipped with window screens simulate various circumstances, from normal flight to the spray of a water landing. Crew members are trained to master aircraft evacuations, CPR, and decisive emergency response, and they cannot fly until they get it right. This relentless commitment to preparedness and service excellence places Cathay Pacific's cabin crew as one of the top ranked crew globally.

## 4. Garuda Indonesia

{{< img src="/06042026/4.png" id="i_plane" caption="Aeroprints.com / Wikimedia" alt="Airbus A330 Garuda Indonesia at Nagoya.">}}

[Garuda Indonesia](https://gitc.garuda-indonesia.com/training-catalog/flight-attendant), an airline from Indonesia, has been recognized by Skytrax among the World's Best Flight Attendants. This airline’s cabin crew undergo an intensive three-to-four-month training program that develops three core competencies: theoretical knowledge, practical service skills in full-scale cabin mock-ups, and mastery of emergency procedures including dry and wet evacuation drills.

Trainees are required to master everything from grooming and proper greeting techniques to food presentation and in-flight service, spending weeks in mock-up aircraft until procedures are fully mastered. Then, they should advance to realistic emergency simulations where they must evacuate a fuselage into a large pool within 90 seconds. 

The airline's renowned experience service philosophy engages all five senses to showcase their origin country’s rich culture and warmth, with rigorous safety standards that require cabin crew to complete recurrent emergency training alongside pilots every 24 months to maintain their certification.

## 5. EVA Air

{{< img src="/06042026/5.png" id="i_plane" caption="Ken Fielding / Wikimedia" alt="Eva Air at Singapore Changi Airport.">}}  
   
As Taiwan’s major 5-star international airline, [EVA Air](https://www.evaair.com/en-global/about-eva-air/about-us/) has built a global reputation for meticulous service and attention to detail, a philosophy aligned with its “5-Star Service from the Ground Up” value. This commitment begins even before a candidate is hired, with a highly selective recruitment process that seeks individuals who embody the airline's exacting standards, with a focus on communication and cultural connection.  
   
Once selected, trainees undergo a rigorous onboarding process and training in simulated, real-facility environments. The training also includes a ["Mind Explorer"](https://www.evaair.com/en-us/about-eva-air/about-us/accessibility-plan/2025-progress-report-hrc/) camp to sharpen the cabin crew’s stress management skills and emotional intelligence. This thorough approach ensures that EVA Air's cabin crew are not only impeccably groomed and professional but also highly disciplined and capable of delivering the consistent, gracious service that has earned the airline numerous accolades and a loyal following among discerning travelers.

## 6. Hainan Airlines

{{< img src="/06042026/6.png" id="i_plane" caption="4300streetcar / Wikimedia" alt="Hainan Airlines Boeing 787-9 B-207V departing Boston June 2025.">}}  
   
[Hainan Airlines](https://www.hainanairlines.com/HUPortal/dyn/portal/DisplayPage?COUNTRY_SITE=US&SITE=CBHZCBHZ&LANGUAGE=US&PAGE=CHAP) is the only Chinese carrier to be consistently rated as a Skytrax 5-Star airline. This airline places a strong emphasis on the holistic development and presentation of its cabin crew, considering flight attendants as brand ambassadors. Their exceptional cabin crew contributes to the worldwide commendation for their premium service quality.   
   
Actively emphasizing foreign language specialty in their flight attendants, including Korean, Japanese, and Russian, Hainan's crew caters to its diverse, international passenger base with linguistic ease and cultural sensitivity. This reinforces the airline's commitment to providing a warm, professional, and globally-minded service at 35,000 feet.

## 7. Japan Airlines

{{< img src="/06042026/7.png" id="i_plane" caption="4300streetcar / Wikimedia" alt="Japan Airlines Boeing 787-9 JA881J on final approach to Boston April 2022.">}}

Carrying refined Japanese hospitality, known as omotenashi, [Japan Airlines](https://www.jal.com/en/company/jal_history/) (JAL) is known for their intuitive, anticipatory, and deeply respectful service. JAL’s cabin crew also undergoes rigorous training to ensure stellar hospitality and emergency handling until they become second nature. Not only that, the cabin crew must also repeat their training every year to maintain their license. JAL's training and facilities for emergency evacuation are also considered to be superior to other carriers.

In 2026, JAL made a significant move that underscores their dedication to service excellence, introducing a new, industry-leading pay structure for JAL’s cabin crew that surpasses its domestic rival ANA. This strategic investment in human capital helps them attract and retain top-tier talent that are not only highly skilled in safety procedures, but also motivated and empowered to deliver the world-class service with grace and precision.

## 8. Malaysia Airlines

{{< img src="/06042026/8.png" id="i_plane" caption="Md Shaifuzzaman / Wikimedia" alt="Malaysia Airlines.">}}  
   
For [Malaysia Airlines](https://www.malaysiaairlines.com/hq/en/about-us.html), the foundation of its renowned cabin crew excellence lies in world-class, continuous training. The airline's training arm, [Malaysia Airlines Academy](https://malaysiaaviationgroup.com.my/en/our-brands-and-services/aviation-services/mab-academy.html) or MAB Academy, is where flight attendants are shaped into some of the best in the industry. Recognized globally, training programs for cabin crew at MAB focuses on high safety standards, cabin service, grooming, etiquette, and emergency procedures.  
   
The Academy's state-of-the-art facilities are crucial to this mission. In early 2026, MAB Academy debuted a new, purpose-built simulator facility which allows realistic and comprehensive emergency procedure drills, including simulated water landings. By combining high-fidelity safety training with a curriculum focused on the warmth and hospitality of Malaysian culture, Malaysian Airlines ensures that their cabin crew are prepared for any situation and excel at making every passenger feel like a welcomed guest.  

## 9. STARLUX Airlines

{{< img src="/06042026/9.png" id="i_plane" caption="4300streetcar / Wikimedia" alt="Starlux Airbus A350-1000 B-58551 departing Taoyuan.">}}

STARLUX Airlines is Taiwan’s boutique carrier founded in 2018, which has distinguished itself as a five-star airline with an exquisite cabin service . With a modern Airbus fleet flying to destinations across Asia-Pacific and North America, STARLUX’s cabin crew philosophy is built on the belief that true hospitality begins with sincerity, and that every genuine connection creates a lasting impression.

Carrying the spirit of "Exquisite in Every Detail", crew members need to undergo months of training, focusing on the art of caring with heart and being people-oriented. In every flight, they deliver thoughtful services that elevate passengers’ comfort, making each journey memorable. This commitment has helped STARLUX become [the fastest airline](https://www.starlux-airlines.com/en-ID/about-us/media-center/news/2025/202506181) to achieve five-star SKYTRAX recognition.

## 10. Korean Air

{{< img src="/06042026/10.png" id="i_plane" caption="Rolf Wallner / Wikimedia" alt="Korean Air in Switzerland">}}  

[Korean Air](https://www.koreanair.com/contents/footer/about-us/who-we-are/overview/introduce-corporation?hl=en) is the country’s flagship carrier primarily based at Seoul Incheon International Airport (ICN). As part of their mission to show service excellence, as well as love for passengers, this airline is known for their warm and empathetic cabin crew.  

After acquiring Asiana Airlines in late 2024, Korean Air has recently initiated integrated training programs for flight attendants from both carriers. By developing a unified curriculum with online education and custom Full Flight Simulator programs, they aim to raise the bar on safety preparedness across their newly expanded workforce, while upholding the airline's elegant and attentive service style. They are also proactively enhancing its protocols, giving passengers complete assurance in how their cabin crew are trained to be the most capable first responders in the sky.
